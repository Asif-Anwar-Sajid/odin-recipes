Savor the exquisite simplicity of pain-au-chocolat, a French pastry that marries layers of flaky, golden-brown goodness with a decadent heart of dark chocolate. The crisp exterior gives way to a delightful dance of textures, creating a moment of indulgence that epitomizes the artistry of French baking in every blissful bite.

Embark on the joyous creation of pain-au-chocolat by layering butter-infused puff pastry with squares of dark chocolate, folding, chilling, and repeating for flaky perfection. Cut into rectangles, let them rise, and bake until golden. The result: a symphony of buttery crispness and molten chocolate, capturing the essence of French pastry artistry in every delightful bite.

To make Baguette, you'll need the following ingredients:

    For the Starter (Poolish):

  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm water
  • 2 cups (250g) bread flour

    • For the Dough:

  • 4 cups (500g) bread flour

    • Others:

  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups (360ml) warm water

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Starter (Poolish):

    Mix yeast with warm water and let it sit for 5 minutes. Add flour and mix until a sticky dough forms. Cover and let it ferment at room temperature for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  2. Activate the yeast:

    Dissolve yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.

  3. Mix the dough:

    In a large bowl, combine bread flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and the prepared poolish. Mix to form a dough.

  4. Knead and rise:

    Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes, then place it in a greased bowl. Cover and let it rise until doubled in size.

  5. Shape the baguette:

    Punch down the dough, divide it into two or three portions, and shape each portion into a long baguette. Place them on a floured surface or a baguette pan.

  6. Proofing:

    Cover the baguettes and let them rise until doubled in size.

  7. Preheat

    Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C). If you have a baking stone, place it in the oven.

  8. Score and bake:

    Before baking, score the baguettes with a sharp knife. If using a baking stone, transfer the baguettes onto it. Bake for about 20-25 minutes or until golden brown and crusty.

  9. Cool and enjoy:

    Allow the pain-au-chocolat to cool slightly before indulging in the flaky layers and gooey chocolate center.

Allow the baguettes to cool before slicing and savoring the crispy crust and soft interior. Perfect for sandwiches or as a side to your favorite meal.
