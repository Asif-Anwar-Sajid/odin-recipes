Savor the exquisite simplicity of pain-au-chocolat, a French pastry that marries layers of flaky, golden-brown goodness with a decadent heart of dark chocolate. The crisp exterior gives way to a delightful dance of textures, creating a moment of indulgence that epitomizes the artistry of French baking in every blissful bite.

Embark on the joyous creation of pain-au-chocolat by layering butter-infused puff pastry with squares of dark chocolate, folding, chilling, and repeating for flaky perfection. Cut into rectangles, let them rise, and bake until golden. The result: a symphony of buttery crispness and molten chocolate, capturing the essence of French pastry artistry in every delightful bite.

To make Pain-Au-Chocolat, you'll need the following ingredients:

    For the Dough:

  • 2 1/4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (30g) sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm milk
  • 1 1/4 cups (280g) unsalted butter, cold

    • For the Filling:

  • High-quality dark chocolate, cut into small squares

    • For Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg, beaten

Steps:

  1. Activate the yeast:

    Dissolve the yeast in warm milk and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.

  2. Mix dry ingredients:

    In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Add the yeast mixture and mix to form a dough.

  3. Chill the dough:

    Cover the dough and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight.

  4. Laminate the dough:

    Roll out the chilled dough into a rectangle. Place cold, thinly sliced butter on two-thirds of the dough. Fold the uncovered third over the butter-covered middle third, then fold the remaining third over the top. Chill, then repeat this rolling and folding process at least two more times.

  5. Assemble:

    Roll out the laminated dough, cut into rectangles, and place squares of dark chocolate on each. Roll them up, ensuring the seam is on the bottom.

  6. Proofing:

    Place the pain-au-chocolat on a baking sheet, cover, and let them proof until doubled in size.

  7. Preheat and egg wash:

    Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Brush the pain-au-chocolat with the beaten egg for a golden finish.

  8. Bake:

    Bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.

  9. Cool and enjoy:

    Allow the pain-au-chocolat to cool slightly before indulging in the flaky layers and gooey chocolate center.
